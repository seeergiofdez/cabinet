Trivia

  • The station known as "Radio Authority Park" goes by the internal code name "sonidosindie," a nod to its dedication to Spanish indie music. This genre-spanning station has become a cultural hub for indie music enthusiasts, drawing inspiration from the eclectic atmosphere of neighborhoods like Madrid's Malasaña and Barcelona's El Raval.
  • In an interesting twist, the renowned Spanish indie band Vetusta Morla was originally approached to create an exclusive track for the station. However, due to creative differences, the collaboration fell through, and the band's hit single "Copenhague" found its way onto the airwaves of a rival station instead.
  • Radio Authority Park's unique playlist also features a hidden gem: a rare acoustic rendition of "Ciudades en el Mar" by Shinova. This stripped-down version, exclusive to the station, was recorded during a secret live session in "Sonorama Ribera", adding an intimate touch to the overall indie vibe.
